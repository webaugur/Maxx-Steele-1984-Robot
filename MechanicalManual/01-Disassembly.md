# Chapter 1 — Disassembly

Follow these steps to open the Maxx Steele chassis for inspection or repair. Complete them in order.

---

## Preparation

1. Move Maxx's arms to the **fully up** position.
2. Turn Maxx **off** and **remove the battery**.

---

## Remove stabilizers

3. Remove Maxx's **front stabilizer**:
   - Unscrew the two screws holding the stabilizer in place.
   - Gently pry the two fingers that slide under the body with a **wide-blade screwdriver**.
   - Place the stabilizer and screws together so they are not separated.
4. Repeat step 3 on the **rear stabilizer**.

---

## Open the main case

5. Lay Maxx **face down** and remove the **twelve screws** holding the back of the case to the front.

---

## Remove the head / remote holder

6. Remove the **remote control holder** from Maxx's head:
   - Remove the screw from the front plate (the plate holding his "eyes").
   - Remove the front plate and the sponge pad behind the eyes.
   - Press gently on the **tang at the rear** of the remote control holder and lift the holder away from the head.
   - **Take care not to strain** the wires going to the remote control holder. Lay the holder to the side.

---

## Separate the back shell

7. Remove the **two case screws** that were hidden by the remote control holder.
8. Remove the **back of the case**, again being careful of the wires from the inside body to the back.
9. Press gently on Maxx's **"armpits"** so the case can clear his **"shoulders"**.

You now have access to the interior. For board-level work, see [`Mainboard/`](../Mainboard/) and [`Face/`](../Face/).
